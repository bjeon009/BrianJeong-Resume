I am a first-year sociology major studying at the University of California, Riverside. Over the past few years, I have interned at the University of California, Irvine, where I assisted in transcribing interviews from families of Holocaust survivors or simply families affected by it. In addition, I interned at Parke Law Firm during the summer of 2018, where I was able to understand what it was like to work in a law firm setting. I also volunteered at the Koreatown Senior Center, where I was able to help underprivileged individuals in a free legal clinic held by volunteer attorneys. I plan on pursuing a career in law once I finish school.

I am 19 years old and was born in Las Vegas, Nevada. I moved to California in 2015, where I attended Troy High School in Fullerton, California. During high school, I was involved in many clubs such as Children's Hospital Orange County, Liberty in North Korea, Christian Club, and a club of which I founded called SHINE, Supporting the Homeless In Need of Essentials. My club collected and distributed daily necessities and toiletries to homeless shelters throughout Orange County. My high school experiences influenced my decision to pursue Sociology at UCR.

Some of my hobbies include spending time with family, eating, exercising, reading, and playing the piano.
